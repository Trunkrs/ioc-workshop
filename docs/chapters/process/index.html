1. Define the process

For this workshop, we’ll be focusing on a vending machine that would only accept card payments. This is to make our solution more straightforward. So imagine you’re standing in front of this beauty on a sunny and hot afternoon.

Process definition#

Like with everything in life it’s very important to understand the problem and then plan the solution from your understanding. So let's start by describing the process and then specifying the requirements from our definition. The following steps are what comes to my mind as a clear definition.

  1. The customer selects their drink preference
  2. The customer sees the price of the drink
  3. The customer pays with their card
  4. The payment is approved by the bank
  5. The machine dispenses the chosen drink

Simple, right! After that, we can start enjoying our ice-cold can of coke!

Business rules#

Now that we know what our process looks like, we can establish the business rules for our process.

  • When the customer selects a drink and the drink is available then show the price otherwise ask the customer to select another drink
  • When the customer selects a drink and the drink is available then activate the payment terminal
  • When the bank has approved the transaction then dispense the drink
  • When the bank rejects the transaction then go back into the initial state And that’s it, not too many rules in this case! It’s still a good idea to map them out for yourself. Even if the scope of the problem seems really small.

Summary#

Now that we understand the business rules and the process we can start making a plan for our architecture. We should always make sure we understand the problem that the business faces before we start making choices about the tech. Remember that using the right tech at the right moment, will make your life a lot easier and will aid in developing value for the company.
